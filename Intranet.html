Definition

An intranet is a private, secure internal network used by organizations to share information, tools, and operational systems exclusively among employees.It acts as a virtual nerve center, providing a centralized hub for employees to access crucial resources, collaborate on projects, share knowledge, and stay informed about the latest updates within the company. In essence, an intranet enhances your workplace connectivity, streamlines workflows, and fosters a culture of collaboration within your business.

Roles

  • Internal Communication: Facilitates targeted messaging to specific departments or roles, reducing communication overload.
  • Knowledge Management & Information Sharing: Acts as a central repository to store, share, and edit documents, ensuring easy access to company knowledge
  • Employee Engagement: Drives interaction through social feeds,Q&A forums, and employee recognition boards.
