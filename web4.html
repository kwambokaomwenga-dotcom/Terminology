Definition

Web 4.0, or the "symbiotic web," is the anticipated next generation of the internet, characterized by proactive, intelligent, and autonomous AI agents that act on behalf of users. It shifts from manual, browser-based interaction to an integrated experience connecting real and virtual worlds through IoT, AI, and edge computing, creating a highly personalized, "invisible" internet

Characteristics

  • AI and Machine Learning Integration: Advanced AI enables predictive analytics, automation, and enhanced decision-making.
  • Ubiquitous Connectivity: Real-time access through 5G, IoT, and edge computing for uninterrupted interactions.
  • Decentralized Framework: Enhanced data privacy and security through blockchain and decentralized networks.
  • Context Awareness: Systems dynamically adapt to user preferences and environmental conditions.

Features

  • Tailored experiences based on user behavior and preferences.
  • AI-driven applications continuously learn and improve from user interactions.
  • Voice, gesture, and brain-computer interfaces for intuitive user control.
  • Real-time decision-making by smart devices and bots.
