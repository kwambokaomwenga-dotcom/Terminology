Definition

An extranet is a controlled, private network that allows authorized external users—such as vendors, partners, or customers—to access specific parts of an organization’s internal intranet. It acts as a secure bridge, often protected by firewalls and passwords, to facilitate communication, collaboration, and data sharing between companies and their external partners.Example:An e-commerce site exchanges information with its retailers, a supplier’s through an extranet network.

Features

  • Data security: Sharing confidential data is of utmost priority and the extranet provides a safe environment for data sharing between the organizations. This decreases the chance of loss of confidential data and increases productivity.
  • Faster communication: Extranet allows to connect multiple organizations and escalate the communication between them
  • Flexibility: Extranet provides a flexible and scalable environment to work on for everyone involved, which also increases the productivity of the organization.
  • Cost: It may decrease the cost of paperwork and travel to some extent.

Advatages and disadvantages

Advantages: security - formed as a Virtual private network(VPN) assures a protected and secure communication, large amount of data can be transferred easily, network sharing - one or more organizations could connect via extranet and communication - medium for internal and external members to connect to third parties.

Disadvantages:expensive - it is costly compared to intranet due to the extra layer of security and hosting charges, limited - it can only be accessed through the internet and complex security - extranet needs an additional firewall if hosted on its own server which expands workload and complex security mechanism.
